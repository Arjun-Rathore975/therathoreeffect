Hi, I’m Arjun Rathore.

I’m studying mechatronics engineering at the University of Sydney, learning how machines sense, think, and act. I’m interested in how mechanical systems and intelligent algorithms come together to solve real problems.

Building an AI drone to detect crop disease taught me that good engineering starts with understanding the problem, not chasing complexity. Researching regeneration in planarian flatworms at INSTEM showed me that biology and engineering share the same logic—feedback, iteration, and resilience. Working on deep-learning models for satellite imagery at Myelin Foundry helped me appreciate the art of data science — learning how to bring order to imperfect data.

These projects shaped how I approach technology—as an iterative process grounded in clarity and purpose. I’m currently exploring how robotics and AI can bridge software and hardware through efficient, human-centered design.

Outside academics, I play football as a goalkeeper, perform drums at live events, and read about innovators who built lasting systems from first principles. Whether it’s music, sport, or robotics, I’ve learned that timing, rhythm, and structure drive everything that works well.
