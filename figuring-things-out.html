Figuring Things Out

I’ve always been more interested in how things work than in making them work perfectly. Engineering, to me, is a way of understanding — taking something apart, figuring out the logic behind it, and putting it back together with a little more clarity each time.

Right now, I’m learning the fundamentals: circuits, control, materials, and motion. It’s humbling how much there is to learn, but that’s what keeps it exciting. Every new concept connects to something I’ve seen before — a pattern, a behavior, or a system that suddenly clicks.

My curiosity doesn’t stay in one lane. Biology taught me how nature builds self-correcting systems. AI showed me how machines learn through failure. Robotics feels like the bridge between them — where software and hardware meet to create motion that makes sense.

I don’t see technology as something to master, but as something to explore. It changes faster than anyone can keep up with, but the underlying questions stay the same: how can we make things more efficient, more reliable, and a little more human?

That’s what keeps me learning — not the end result, but the process of figuring it out piece by piece.
